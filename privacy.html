5. CREATOR VOICE CLONING AND BIOMETRIC INFORMATION

5.1 Overview of Biometric Information

Biometric information refers to physiological or biological characteristics that can be used to uniquely identify an individual. In the context of Soqratic Services, biometric information consists of voiceprints and voice characteristics extracted from audio recordings.

Important Distinction: Biometric information does not include photographs, videos, or audio recordings themselves, but rather the unique mathematical representations or templates derived from such recordings.

5.2 Scope of Biometric Collection

Biometric information is collected exclusively from Creators who voluntarily choose to use voice cloning features. We do not collect biometric information from Clients or general platform users.

From Creators (Optional - Voice Cloning Feature):

  • Voiceprints: Mathematical representations of vocal characteristics including pitch, tone, rhythm, intonation, accent, and speech patterns
  • Facial geometry data: Measurements and characteristics of facial features (if visual representation features are implemented)

From Clients (Voice Input):

  • No biometric identifiers are created
  • Audio is immediately transcribed to text using speech-to-text services
  • Audio recordings are permanently deleted after transcription
  • No voiceprints or biometric templates are extracted or stored

5.6 Biometric Data Protection

Biometric information receives enhanced security measures beyond our standard protections:

Technical Safeguards:

  • AES-256 encryption for all stored biometric data and voice models
  • TLS 1.3 encryption for all biometric data transmission
  • Isolated storage systems with restricted access controls
  • Multi-factor authentication required for personnel accessing biometric data systems
  • Comprehensive access logging and monitoring
  • Regular security audits specifically covering biometric data handling

Organizational Safeguards:

  • Access strictly limited to authorized personnel with legitimate business need
  • Background checks for employees with biometric data access
  • Confidentiality and non-disclosure agreements
  • Specialized training on biometric data handling and sensitivity
  • Documented incident response procedures for biometric data breaches

Third-Party Processor Requirements:

When biometric data is shared with voice synthesis providers:

  • Providers must execute comprehensive Data Processing Agreements
  • Providers must implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures
  • Providers may only use biometric data for authorized voice model creation purposes
  • Providers are prohibited from using biometric data for their own model training or other purposes
  • Providers must comply with applicable biometric privacy laws
  • Providers must delete biometric data when voice cloning features are terminated

5.7 Biometric Information Retention

During Active Account: Biometric information is retained for the duration of Creator account while voice cloning features remain active.

After Account Termination: Biometric information is retained for up to 90 days following account termination to allow for potential account reactivation or technical issue resolution.

After Grace Period: Biometric information is permanently and irretrievably deleted following the 90-day grace period.

Backup Systems: Deleted biometric information may remain in encrypted backup systems for up to 180 days following deletion. Backup data is not accessible for operational use and is automatically purged according to backup retention schedules.

Jurisdiction-Specific Retention:

  • Illinois (BIPA): For Illinois residents, biometric information will be permanently deleted within 3 years of last interaction with Soqratic, or when the initial purpose for collection is satisfied, whichever occurs first
  • Other jurisdictions: Compliance with applicable local retention requirements

Legal Holds: If required by law, court order, or legal process, biometric information may be retained beyond stated periods. Users will be notified when legally permissible.

Permanent Deletion Process:

When retention periods expire, biometric information is permanently deleted through:

  • Multiple overwrites of data with random values
  • Cryptographic key destruction rendering encrypted data unrecoverable
  • Removal of all database records and references
  • Deletion from all active systems and caches
  • Purging from backup systems according to backup retention schedule
  • Verification of deletion from third-party processors

7. COOKIES AND TRACKING TECHNOLOGIES

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit our website or use our Services. We also use similar technologies including web beacons, pixels, local storage, and session tokens.

Essential Cookies (Strictly Necessary)

  • Purpose: Required for Services to function properly
  • Examples:
  • Authentication cookies to maintain login sessions
  • Session management to remember actions during browsing session
  • Security tokens to prevent cross-site request forgery attacks
  • Load balancing to distribute traffic across servers
  • Duration: Session-based (deleted when browser closes) or up to 1 year
  • User Control: Cannot be disabled without breaking core functionality
  • Legal Basis (GDPR): Legitimate interests / Technical necessity (Article 6(1)(f))

Functional Cookies (Preferences)

  • Purpose: Remember user choices and preferences to enhance experience
  • Examples:
  • Language preference settings
  • User interface theme selections (light mode, dark mode)
  • Bot interaction preferences
  • Notification settings
  • Duration: 30 days to 1 year
  • User Control: Can be disabled; may result in need to re-enter preferences each visit
  • Legal Basis (GDPR): Consent (Article 6(1)(a)) for EU/UK users

Performance and Analytics Cookies (Optional)

  • Purpose: Understand how users interact with Services to identify improvement opportunities
  • Information Collected:
  • Pages viewed and navigation paths
  • Features utilized and interaction patterns
  • Time spent on pages and overall session duration
  • Errors encountered and system performance
  • Examples:
  • Google Analytics (if implemented)
  • Internal analytics systems
  • Duration: 30 days to 2 years
  • User Control: Can be disabled via:
  • Browser settings
  • Cookie consent preferences (EU/UK users)
  • Google Analytics opt-out: https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout
  • Legal Basis (GDPR): Consent (Article 6(1)(a)) for EU/UK users; legitimate interests for others
  • Important Limitation: We do not track users across different websites (no cross-site tracking)

  • Advertising or marketing cookies for targeted advertisements
  • Retargeting or remarketing cookies that follow users across websites
  • Cross-site tracking cookies
  • Social media tracking pixels (except when explicitly using social login features)
  • Third-party marketing or advertising network cookies
